All you need to know about psoriasis

All you need to know about psoriasis

Psoriasis is a disease that requires severe restrictions in many ways. But you can not just feel down not to make yourself even worse.
A person with advanced stage of psoriasis looks unattractive: on his face, body and head appear scabs - dead skin pells. Nevertheless, it does not make any danger to others. But for a patient there is a serious danger.
Psoriasis, which many mistakenly consider it as a skin disease, can affect the kidneys, joints, heart and blood vessels. But this systemic ailment affects almost the whole organism. In addition, it increases the risk of type 2 diabetes and in severe forms reduces lifertime by an average of 5-7 years.
Reasons of the disease are still not clear. However, it is proved that an important role is played by: heredity, stress, infection, taking of some medicines. Therefore, it is very important to protect yourself and children from all these risks, especially if there is a genetic predisposition to the disease.
The diagnosis is most often based on external manifestations. With the detection of psoriatic arthritis, everything is much more difficult and sometimes doctors can not determine it for a year or more, that is very sad because the prognosis depends largely on the stage of the disease, as well as on the reaction of a body to treatment and a lifestyle that a patient is leading.
To successfully control psoriasis, first of all you need to avoid stresses (or rather, learn to react to them correctly without taking everything to heart).
The diet should be balanced. To avoid exacerbations it is important not to overeat and reduce the amount of fatty foods. Salt minimum. Alcohol and smoking are banned.
Those who suffer from psoriasis, the idea of ​​the benefits of physical exercise, usually comes to mind at the end of the line. However, studies have shown that people who engage in sports suffer from severe illness and exacerbation rare. After all, physical activity neutralizes stress, pressure and depression, which often worsen the course of psoriasis. Choosing a type of activity depends on age and concomitant pathology. It can be: swimming, walking, running, yoga, pilates, cycling. They improve blood circulation, strengthen the cardiovascular system and relieve stress.
In addition to local and systemic treatment, you need constant care of your skin and nails. Taking a shower or bath you need to use a soft sponge or a cotton napkin, not a bast wisp. Hard soap, gasoline and other solvents for skin cleansing are prohibited.
It is important not to allow skin to dry up and chap. To do this during washing it is necessary to use a means enriched with lipids which do not cause irritation and dryness. After showering, apply a softening, moisturizing remedy (cream or lotion) to skin to restore the hydrolipid mantle of skin to prevent its dehydration. softening products should be used at least once a day.
It is important to shorten nails shortly not to injure skin because damage during scratching plaques can cause new rashes.
The right treatment is very important. It depends on the stage and form of psoriasis. Today there are innovative drugs - so-called biological therapy.
The psychological attitude is no less important. Of course the disease is not an easy test. And it's not just a painful symptom. Misunderstanding and fear of getting infected by others, problems in personal life, complexes about appearance - that's what the patient has to face every day. There are also cases of discrimination. For example, sometimes a child with psoriasis simply exclude from the kindergarten parents of other healthy children.
